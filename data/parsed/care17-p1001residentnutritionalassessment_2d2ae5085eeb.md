## POLICY
### Nutritional Care and Hydration

## PROCEDURE

### UPON MOVE-IN OF THE RESIDENT:
- The nurse must initiate a **Nutrition - Communication to Culinary Department** tool, stating the diet, texture, and liquid (fluid) consistency the resident is to receive based on move-in paperwork, along with any other nutrition-related prescribed orders such as oral nutrition supplements. The nurse will process the resident’s orders for pharmacy, indicating the resident’s diet, texture, liquid consistency and applicable supplements and doses, and will enter them into the resident’s Medication Administration Record (MAR).
- The nurse will document a minimum fluid goal of **12 units per day (1500 ml/d)**, or any fluid restrictions or other contraindications, in the profile of the resident’s medical record until Registered Dietitian assessment.
- The nurse must initiate a **Referral - Registered Dietitian**.

### ADMISSION ASSESSMENT
- The Culinary Manager (CM) or designate must process any “in progress” **Nutrition - Communication to Culinary Department** tools and address the request(s), including the creation of any new resident profiles in the food service software, as applicable.
- The CM/designate must visit the resident(s) and complete a **Nutrition - Move-In Profile** for each new resident following InterRAI assessment timelines.
- The Registered Dietitian (RD)/designate must complete a **Nutrition - Assessment** and document an individualized fluid goal in the profile section of the medical chart for each new resident according to InterRAI assessment timelines.
- The RD must process any “in progress” **Referral - Registered Dietitian** tools related to the new resident(s), as applicable.

**Note:**
- When the home RD is unable to be onsite to assess residents, the home team must follow **Guidelines for Registered Dietitian Remote Services**.
- When there is no home RD, the designate must be a registered staff member who completes any applicable assessments and documentation.
- In Manitoba: Admission nutrition assessments must be completed for all residents within eight weeks of admission.

### INITIAL PLAN OF CARE
- The RD/designate must document in the interdisciplinary plan of care within InterRAI timelines.
- The RD documents must identify nutrition condition(s) or concern(s) in the appropriate foci.

## NUTRITION RISK SCREENING

- The RD/CM/designate must complete a Nutrition - Risk Screen tool after completing the nutrition assessment of a resident. The RD/designate will also complete a Nutrition – Risk Screen tool for any significant change in status warranting a change in risk to the resident’s nutritional status.
- All residents must be assigned a nutritional risk level using the Nutrition - Risk Screen: low, moderate, or high.

**Note:**
- When there is no home RD, the registered nurse/designate must ensure each resident’s nutrition risk is reviewed and updated following InterRAI assessment timelines.

----

## SCHEDULED ROUTINE QUARTERLY OR SIGNIFICANT CHANGE IN STATUS ASSESSMENTS

- The RD/designate must assess the resident(s), complete any applicable assessments, review and update the plan of care, as applicable, and complete appropriate documentation.
- The RD/designate may delegate residents who are at low or moderate nutritional risk to the CM/designate as appropriate.
- The CM/designate must assess any resident(s) delegated by the RD, complete any applicable assessments, refer to the RD for any changes to the resident’s nutritional status or to the plan of care, and complete applicable documentation.
- The RD must discontinue/write new diet orders as required. The nurse must process the orders for pharmacy and enter the new orders into the resident’s MAR.
- The RD/designate must discuss changes in the plan of care with the resident/SDM/POA and interdisciplinary team and notify the CM.
- CM/designate must update the resident Nutrition Profile(s) in the food service software as indicated by the RD or per resident requests.

**Note:**
- When the home RD is unable to be onsite to assess residents, the home team will follow Guidelines for Registered Dietitian Remote Services.
- When there is no home RD, the designate must be a registered staff member who completes any applicable assessments and documentation.
- N/DM/designates should only be delegated resident assessments for those deemed at low or moderate nutritional risk by the home RD and if they have the appropriate educational requirements and credentials.

## CHANGES IN RESIDENT CONDITION

- The interdisciplinary team must communicate any significant changes in the resident’s nutritional status to the RD by initiating a Referral - Registered Dietitian.
- The RD must address referrals based on risk priority and within a reasonable time frame.
- The RD must assess the resident and make applicable recommendations to address risks to nutrition and hydration. Recommendations must be discussed with the resident/SDM/POA and the interdisciplinary team.
- The RD must complete appropriate documentation, discontinue/write prescribed orders as required, and update the resident’s plan of care, accordingly.
- The RD must provide a summary of intervention changes to the CM.
- The CM/designate must update resident Profile(s) in the food service software, as applicable.

**Note:**
- When the home RD is unable to be onsite to assess residents, the home team must follow Guidelines for Registered Dietitian Remote Services.
- When there is no home RD, the designate must be a registered staff member who completes any applicable assessments and documentation.

----

## COMMUNICATION TO CULINARY DEPARTMENT

- The interdisciplinary team must use the Nutrition - Communication to Culinary Department tool to communicate to the CM:
- Resident/SDM/POA requests.
- Changes in resident diet, food texture, liquid consistency.
- Changes in nutrition supplementation, enteral feeding.
- Changes in room, dining area or table assignment requests.
- Requests for assistive (feeding) devices.
- The CM/designate must process any “in progress” Nutrition - Communication to Culinary Department tools and inform the RD of any pertinent changes.
